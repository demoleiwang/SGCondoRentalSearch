### One new nit

- **(P2) `F010` P75 is often labeled as "ceiling" but is actually median-of-top-half.** Marcus expects "P75" = "75% of comparable listings rent at or below this". The app doesn't explain that. A 1-line tooltip on the 🎯 would remove the ambiguity for non-data-literate users who'd otherwise misread "bargaining target" as something more aggressive.

## Lesson for next run

A "trends" feature doesn't need historical time-series to deliver value when a single-quarter dataset already carries percentiles. Surfacing P25/P75 + district-relative position at row level turned what was a mostly-docs feature into a scannable at-a-glance negotiation signal. **Generalization:** when a raw dataset is richer than the UI exposes, the highest-leverage improvement is often "surface an existing column" rather than "acquire more data".
