## Context

- 34, management consultant at a big-4 firm, relocating from Hong Kong with her partner.
- Household take-home: ~SGD 22k/mo (both earn well; employer housing allowance $5k).
- Budget: $6,000–$8,500/mo for a 2BR or small 3BR, walking distance to Orchard / River Valley.
- No car intended — both walk or Grab everywhere. MRT is a safety net, not primary.
- Lease starts in 6 weeks; viewings happen in a single 3-day window so shortlisting has to be tight.
- Spreadsheet-native. Routinely exports data to compare options in Excel/Google Sheets.

## Search Style

- Crisp English, uses technical terms accurately: "3BR freehold Orchard $7-8k high floor", "District 9/10 condo 2 bath walking to Ion".
- Expects power-user features: sort by multiple columns, export to CSV, filter by tenure (freehold/leasehold), year built.
- Tests edge cases early — deliberately submits malformed or ambiguous queries to probe handling.
- Will click through to PropertyGuru but only after the app has narrowed the universe to ~10 candidates.

## Success Criteria

1. Full-data-table + export for offline analysis.
2. Meaningful sort options: rent, $/psf, rent change vs last quarter, distance-to-Orchard-MRT, lease type.
3. Handles queries like "show me only freehold projects with at least 30 contracts" (data credibility filter).
4. Price transparency at the high end: is $8k/mo a floor or ceiling for 2BR in D9?

## Quit Triggers

- No export / copy-table. Forces screenshot + retype.
- Sort is a single dimension; can't break ties intelligently.
- Showing "Downtown 2br 7000" returns a bunch of $3k District 3 condos because of loose district expansion.
- Tool assumes I'm price-sensitive and hides the upper-tier options.
